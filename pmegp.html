About PMEGP

The Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) is a flagship scheme aimed at creating sustainable self-employment opportunities through the establishment of micro-enterprises. It offers credit-linked subsidies and simplified loan processes for eligible entrepreneurs, especially youth, artisans, and small businesses, in both rural and urban areas.

Key Benefits

Financial Support

Includes working capital and term loans with minimal collateral, often covered under credit guarantee schemes, making financing easier for new entrepreneurs.

Generous Subsidy

Provides margin money subsidy covering 15%–35% of project cost (depending on category and location), reducing the repayment burden on beneficiaries.

Marketing Support

Grants access to KVIC marketing outlets, fairs, and exhibitions for beneficiaries to showcase and sell products, enhancing market reach.

Local Employment

Encourages entrepreneurship in rural and urban areas alike, creating jobs locally and curbing mass migration by uplifting communities.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Individual applicants must be 18 years or older.
  • No income ceiling for applicants setting up new micro-enterprises.
  • Minimum VIII standard education required for projects above ₹10L (manufacturing) or ₹5L (service sector).
  • Only new projects specifically sanctioned under PMEGP are eligible.
  • Self-Help Groups, Societies, Cooperatives, and Trusts are eligible if they meet scheme criteria.
  • Only one member of a family (self or spouse) can be a beneficiary under the scheme.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card and PAN Card (identity proof).
  • Caste/Minority/Women/Other special category certificates (if applicable).
  • Residence certificate (for rural projects as defined under the scheme).
  • Detailed Project Report outlining the business plan.
  • Educational or Skill Development certificates (especially for larger projects).
  • Recent passport-size photographs of the applicant.
