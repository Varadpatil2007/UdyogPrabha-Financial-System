What is Simple Interest (A = P(1 + rt))?

Simple interest is the cost of borrowing money or the return on investment over a fixed period. It is calculated on the original principal only.

Whenever someone borrows money (called the principal amount), they repay it with an additional amount known as interest over time.

📘 Simple Interest Formula

A = P(1 + rt)

  • A: Total Amount (Principal + Interest)
  • P: Principal amount
  • r: Annual interest rate (in decimal form, r = R/100)
  • t: Time period in years

🔧 What Is a Simple Interest Calculator?

A simple interest calculator is an online tool that helps you compute interest and total amount quickly. Input the required values and the calculator applies the formula automatically.

It provides accurate, fast, and useful insights for planning loans, savings, or investments.

⚙️ How Does It Work?

To use the calculator, enter:

  • Principal (P): Initial investment or loan amount
  • Interest Rate (r): Annual rate in percentage
  • Time (t): Duration of investment/loan in years

It then calculates:

  • Total Interest Earned
  • Total Amount (A): Principal + Interest

📊 Example:

P = ₹10,000, r = 10%, t = 6 years

A = 10,000 × (1 + 0.1 × 6) = ₹16,000

Interest = ₹6,000

✅ Benefits of Using a Simple Interest Calculator

  • 🎯 Convenience: Easy to use for everyone
  • ⚡ Quick & Accurate: Instant results with reliable calculations
  • 📈 Compare Schemes: Evaluate various investment/loan options
  • 📅 Financial Planning: Understand future returns or obligations clearly
